29.

Public Questions

A period of up to 30 minutes during which any resident of the Borough or representative of organisations within the Borough (other than members of the Council) may ask a question on matters relevant to the Committee.

Advance notice of questions is not required, but is encouraged in order to ensure an answer may be given on the night if the question is of a technical nature.

Minutes:

 

 

A resident asked what measures were in place to ensure HMO’s are licensed and meet safety standards. The resident asked further, how residents would be informed about HMO’s in their neighbourhood. Members of the committee advised the resident that one would need a licence to operate your HMO only for any property with 5 or more unrelated occupiers, forming more than one household and sharing amenities;

 

A resident asked if there was an article 4 for HMO’s in Kingston to potentially protect residents. Members advised the Council was currently looking into this.

34.

Community Police Update

Minutes:

A member of the Metropolitan Police reported. There had been a crime spike during October with increases in burglaries and motor vehicle theft. His team were personally visiting each victim of burglary and vehicle theft. They also visited neighbours to see if there were any witnesses or if there was any CCTV footage they might have. The burglary spike had dropped off and there had been a couple of arrests including an arrest for a residential burglary that happened in Chessington where jewelry was stolen. There had also been a recent arrest for theft of motor vehicles. One of their priorities of was antisocial behavior particularly that caused by vehicles. They had been working with Citizen Zoo at Tolworth Court Farm and had almost entirely stopping access

to those on two wheels getting into the farm, which had really helped the drop of antisocial behavior in the whole King George’s and Sunray ward. They had conducted a warrant in Chessington South for some drugs with a good result. An arrest was made and they had recovered 4 kg of cannabis, some class A drugs, a samurai sword, another nasty looking knife, and other items. There had been a stabbing during October outside the KFC in Bridge Road which was in the hands of CRD. The victim was not local to the area and was recovering well in hospital.

To consider applications for Neighbourhood Community Grants (NCGs)

Additional documents:

  • 9_Annex 1 - NCG and BCG Guidelines (updated May 2023) , item 35. PDF 91 KB

Decision:

RESOLVED that:

 

1.    a Neighbourhood Community Grant to Man & Boy to support their Stronger Families Kingston and North Kingston project, for the sum of £2,230.00 be approved, and

 

2.    a Neighbourhood Community Grant to Oxygen to support their mentoring programme, for the sum of £2,230.00 be approved.

 

3.    a Neighbourhood Community Grant to the Kingston Tamil Heritage Group to support the Kingston Tamil Heritage and Harvest Festival which is taking place on Sunday 25 January 2026 at Richard Challoner School in New Malden, for the sum of £825.00 be approved.

 

Minutes:

The committee received 3 applications for Neighbourhood Community Grants.

 

The committee noted that there was currently £5,285 remaining in the Kingston and North Kingston Neighbourhood Community Grant fund, which was insufficient to fund all 3 Grants should the Committee decide to award them in full.

 

Each applicant was given an opportunity to present their application after which the committee decided on the allocation of the grants:

 

The first application for £3,000.00 was from Man&Boy, a local charitable organisation established in light of the significant number of vulnerable children and families that suffer from family separation, absent parents and limited local community support networks in Kingston. Man&Boy supports families that have experienced relationship breakdown to reach positive outcomes and improved family relations through inclusive camping, enrichment activities courses, and mentoring.

 

Man & Boy applied for £3,000.00 Neighbourhood Community Grant to support their Stronger Families Kingston and North Kingston project which aimed to deliver focused support to 6 referred beneficiaries from Kingston and North Kingston wards.

 

The second application for a £3,000.00 Neighbourhood Community Grant was from Oxygen, a registered charity based in Kingston, which provides a range of services, support, and interventions aimed at changing the lives of marginalised, disadvantaged and isolated young people. Oxygen was established by local young people and seeks to meet young people on their own terms, to understand the world from their perspective and help them overcome the challenges they face.

 

The Grant would help them to support their mentoring programme enabling them to employ and sustain both their qualified staff and volunteers so that they can respond to the growing mental health needs of marginalised, socio-economically deprived and vulnerable young people in the Kingston and North Kingston. It would enable Oxygen to sustain, develop and manage their volunteers, many of whom are young people undertaking social action. Oxygen was also applying for the same grant from the other 3 Neighbourhood Committees.

 

Procedure Rule 27 ATTENDANCE OF MEMBERS AT COMMITTEES

 

The Chair informed the Committee that Councillor Thay Thayalan had indicated his intention to co-present the Kingston Tamil Heritage Group Neighbourhood Community Grant application in accordance with Procedure Rule 27.

The Committee gave its permission to Councillor Thayalan to co-present the item.

 

The third application for a £1,100.00 Neighbourhood Community Grant was from the Kingston Tamil Heritage Group, which comprises four prominent Tamil organisations: the Centre for Community Development, Tamil Information Centre, ITC Kingston Tamil School, and Surrey Tamil School. These organizations have a strong and enduring presence, having served the Tamil community in Kingston for several decades.

 

They were applying to the Kingston and North Kingston Neighbourhood Committee, as well as the New & Old Malden and Surbiton Neighbourhood Committees, for a £1,100 award (a combined £3,300) to support the Kingston Tamil Heritage and Harvest Festival that is to take place on Sunday 25 January 2026 at Richard Challoner School in New Malden.
