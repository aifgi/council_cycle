59.

Petition Regarding the Introduction of Traffic Management Measures On Manorgate Road PDF 161 KB

To address a petition received seeking a solution to congestion, conflict, speeding and through traffic on Manorgate Road.

Additional documents:

  • Annex 1 - Wolverton Avenue , item 59. PDF 601 KB
  • Annex 2 - Manorgate Road , item 59. PDF 1 MB
  • Annex 3 - Overview plan , item 59. PDF 602 KB
  • Annex 4 - Survey summary , item 59. PDF 353 KB

Decision:

RESOLVED that:

 

1.    the introduction of traffic management measures on Manorgate Road at the existing width restriction and Wolverton Avenue using an experimental traffic management order (ETMO) under sections 9 and 10 of the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984(a), with Manorgate Road to be made one-way Northbound (towards Kingston Hill/roundabout) and Wolverton Avenue to be made one-way between Kingston Hill and Coombe Road, as shown in Annex 3, be approved;

 

2.    a report be brought back to this Committee after 6 months from the implementation of the scheme outlining the outcome of the scheme and the impact on the surrounding roads;

 

3.    approval be granted to proceed with the implementation of a green man phase at the junction of Galworthy Road/Coombe Lane West/Gloucester Road/Coombe Road once funding is secured.

Minutes:

The Chair moved this item up the agenda with the agreement of the Chair.

 

The Committee considered a report outlining a proposed Experimental Traffic Management Order (ETMO) with Manorgate Road to be made one-way Northbound (towards Kingston Hill/roundabout) and Wolverton Avenue to be made one-way between Kingston Hill and Coombe Road. The Committee was informed that a petition, received on July 11th, 2024, with 60 signatures, requested a solution to traffic problems on Manorgate Road.

 

The report detailed the existing traffic issues in the area, outlined available options and sought the Committee's views, comments and approval to implement an experimental traffic management order. This order would allow a trial of the proposed options, with a follow-up report to be presented to this Committee six months after implementation.

 

The report also responded to a repeated request for pedestrian facilities at the

junction of Galsworthy Road/ Coombe Lane West/Gloucester Road/Coombe Road. The Council had had aspirations to improve pedestrian facilities at the Galsworthy Road/Coombe Lane West/Gloucester Road/Coombe Road junction for some years. The junction straddled two neighbourhoods and therefore the New and Old Malden Neighbourhood Committee would be consulted on the proposal at a meeting in January 2026.

 

It was noted that for several years, residents in Manorgate Road had expressed significant concerns about the volume and type of traffic using this road. It was the residents’ perception that current traffic patterns were incompatible with the characteristics of a "home zone", which they understood to mean a residential area designed for shared use by pedestrians, cyclists and motorists featuring traffic calming measures to prioritise safety and community above through traffic.

 

Residents cited conflict between road users and an unacceptable level of risk to all road users and pedestrians. To address this, the Manorgate Road residents asked the Council to implement new measures to reduce through traffic and better reflect the existing “home zone” designation. A formal petition, with 60 signatories, had been submitted to Kingston Council asking for a resolution to the joint issues of through traffic and excessive vehicle speeds in Manorgate Road. Residents had asked the Council to implement traffic calming measures which will improve safety, reduce noise pollution, and contribute to a better quality of life for residents in the Manorgate Road area.

 

Modelling had been undertaken for the Galsworthy Road area using Transport for London’s “ONE model” to project conditions for the year 2026. The study evaluated how different road management strategies, such as the closure of Manorgate Road and the implementation of all-round pedestrian phases, would influence vehicle routing and congestion during morning peak hours. Results indicated that shutting specific routes forced traffic to re-route towards major thoroughfares like Kingston Hill and Coombe Road, often leading to increased queue lengths on surrounding streets. However, the authors cautioned that these findings were indicative rather than absolute, as the model’s accuracy had not been specifically verified for the immediate vicinity of the local junctions.     60.

    Coombe Lane West Zebra Crossing - Local Consultation Results PDF 202 KB

    To consider the results of a public consultation on the Introduction of Zebra Crossing on Coombe Lane West and seek a way forward.

    Additional documents:

    • Annex 1 - Coombe Lane West , item 60. PDF 305 KB

    Decision:

    RESOLVED that the decision on the implementation of the scheme as shown in Annex 1 be deferred to the Committee meeting in March 2026.

    Minutes:

    The Chair moved this item up the agenda with the agreement of the Chair.

     

    The Committee considered a report detailing the proposal to install a new zebra crossing on Coombe Lane West, specifically outside house numbers 54/56. Coombe Lane West formed the boundary with New and Old Malden Neighbourhood, and that committee considered this report in December 2025 and approved the scheme.

     

    The proposal addressed feedback from local residents requesting an improved road crossing. Following site visits and observations, a zebra crossing was proposed to provide a formal and safe crossing point on Coombe Lane West, supporting the council’s wider initiative to encourage walking to school.

     

    The report outlined the results of the local consultation conducted regarding the implementation of the new zebra crossing at this location. In September 2025, consultation letters were sent out to 82 residents within the Coombe Lane West consultation area. The consultation ran from 30 September to 28 October 2025. 50 residents responded to the consultation with 60% of respondents supportive of the introduction of a new zebra crossing in the proposed location and 36% against.

     

    The Committee was informed that over the last five years, six collisions had occurred on Coombe Lane West, specifically between the east of Dickerage Road and Crown Road. These incidents included two serious and four slight injuries.

     

    Residents addressed the Committee to outline their concerns with the proposed location and especially highlighting concerns with the design of the zig zag lines, especially as they went across 6 driveways which would have to manoeuvre illegally on the zig zag lines with poor visibility. It was also noted that this part of the road experienced persistent flooding. The residents proposed a different location especially as school children often used the south bound pavement. 

     

    With the agreement of the Committee, Councillor Giles spoke under procedural rule 27 highlighting that the New and Old Malden Committee had approved the scheme and residents were also supportive of the crossing. He noted that the scheme would encourage walking to school and give pedestrians more opportunities to cross on a high traffic road.

     

    The Committee commented on how the location was chosen and it was emphasised that it had been chosen looking at the data and it had also been requested by residents and local councillors. It was noted that the exact design details would follow.

     

    Regarding the concern that residents would be fined for manoeuvring on zig zag lines, it was noted that there were many places in the borough where residents had zig zag lines over their driveways and there had not been an issue with fines being received.

     

    A proposal was made by the Chair to defer the decision to the March committee meeting pending further research by the Highways department on the legality and clarification on the issue raised of manoeuvring on zig zag lines. The proposal was seconded by Councillor Sadr-Hashemi, voted on and carried.

    61.

    Community Safety Update PDF 10 MB

    To update the Committee on the community safety work that has been undertaken in the last six months and the plans for the next six months. The presentation is published as a supplement alongside the agenda.

     

    (For information only)

    Minutes:

    The Chair moved this item up the agenda with the agreement of the Chair.

     

    The Committee received a verbal presentation on the Council’s community safety work that has been undertaken in the last six months and the plans for the next six months. The presentation was published as a supplement alongside the agenda.

     

    The presentation covered antisocial behaviour (ASB) reports made to the Council, efforts to raise community awareness on ASB reporting and case review, collaboration with other partners on ASB, work on violence against women and girls (VAWG) including domestic abuse, community engagement work on ASB, the white ribbon accreditation and hate crime. It also detailed the training, engagement and community session that had taken place as well as events.

     

    The Committee was also informed that the Community Safety Survey 2025/26 was now live, which asked residents, visitors, staff, and students aged 16 and over in Kingston to share their views on how safe they felt in the borough.

     

    The Committee was very supportive of the work being done in this area and commented on the usefulness of the flyers that told residents how to report ASB and requested a PDF copy that members could circulate to residents electronically. Furthermore, it was noted that some displacement activity would sometimes take place when ASB had been tackled in one area. The Committee was informed that it was a complex issue and often hard to predict where it would go. The design of an area sometimes attracted ASB and funding and uptake from partners was required to change the design and make meaningful impact.

    63.

    Community Grants Programme PDF 170 KB

    To consider applications for Neighbourhood Community Grants (NCGs).

    Additional documents:

    • Annex 1 - NCG and BCG Guidelines (updated May 2023) , item 63. PDF 138 KB

    Decision:

    RESOLVED that:

     

    1.    the redistribution of any remaining Councillor Ward Funding (CWF) as of January 30th 2026 to the Neighbourhood Community Grants Programme be approved; and

     

    2.    the application for £1,800 of Neighbourhood Community Grant funding by Barnfield Riding for the Disabled be approved.

    Minutes:

    The Committee considered a report outlining the proposed redistribution of unspent Councillor Ward Funding (CWF) with any funds unspent by January 30th 2026 being redistributed to the Neighbourhood Community Grants (NCG) Programme in line with the Councillors Ward Funding Guidelines 2022/23.

     

    The Committee was informed that it had already awarded £17,425 of the NCG fund for the year. The pending application in the report amounted to another £1,800. If the application was successful, there would be £775 remaining for the rest of the year. (£20,000 + £989 returned NCG funds from Dowler court)

     

    The Committee also considered one application for a Neighbourhood Community Grant. This grant applicant has also applied for funding for their work in other neighbourhoods within the borough.

     

    Barnfield Riding for the Disabled Association had applied for £1,800 of funding and proposed running weekly therapeutic horse-riding sessions for children with physical and learning disabilities in Kingston. The project would run from February 26 to July 16 2026 offering two cohorts of six children each (12 children total) the opportunity to participate in structured sessions that would include grooming, mounted exercises, and games.

     

    The Committee was supportive of the application being well aware of the applicant and the work they do.

     

    It was RESOLVED that:

     

    1.    the redistribution of any remaining Councillor Ward Funding (CWF) as of January 30th 2026 to the Neighbourhood Community Grants Programme be approved; and

     

    Voting: unanimous

     

    2.    the application for £1,800 of Neighbourhood Community Grant funding by Barnfield Riding for the Disabled be approved.

     

    Voting: unanimous

     

    Councillors Davey, Chohan and Hamed were not present for these votes.
